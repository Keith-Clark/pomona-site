Flashboard Risers

A Flashboard riser is a corrugated aluminum half pipe that is vertically oriented. Each side of the open half of the flashboard riser has a channel guide.Wooden or aluminum stop logs can be stacked in the channel guides to obtain any desired water level up stream of the stop logs. A drain pipe is inserted near the bottom of the flashboard riser that drains the water when it flows over the top of the highest stop log. The water levels can be manipulated from high to low or vice versa simply by adding or taking out stop logs. Stop logs are usually approximately 6" high and up to 42" long. Flashboard risers wider than 42" in width are usually furnished with two or more sets of stop logs depending on the width of the flashboard riser.

Pond Risers

Vertical outlet pipes (also called "risers") are used to set the primary spillway water elevation in a pond or water impondment. The trash guard shown on top of this riser forces the water in the pond to flow under the trash rack and at the same time blocks floating debris from discharging through the top of the riser. The inverted elbow shown on the riser is a mechanism by which the top portion of the impoundment can be discharged over a long period of time. Permanent pond risers and their outlet discharge pipes are usually specified Aluminum which has excellent corrosion resistance. Trash Racks

Trash Racks can be installed wherever your storm drainage system discharges to trap trash and debris, and prevent these pollutants from entering a water body. Our trash racks are manufactured using Aluminum Rebar or Corrugated Aluminum Pipe and can have a variety of shapes and/or sizes to fit on almost any structure.
