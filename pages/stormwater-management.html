Underground Detention & Infiltration

Runoff storage and/or time-release discharge.

Our underground systems offer you flexibility and customization for either detention or infiltration applications – metal, concrete, and plastic systems are available.

Detention systems are employed on a site to reduce the quantity of stormwater runoff leaving a site by temporarily storing the runoff that exceeds a site’s allowable discharge rate, and releasing it slowly over time.

Infiltration systems are incorporated to reduce the volume of stormwater runoff being discharged from a site. This runoff reduction strategy is a major part of a low impact development design.

Stormwater Detention and Infiltration

DETENTION

Our corrugated Aluminum detention systems store stormwater runoff exceeding a site’s allowable discharge rate and release it slowly over time. Installed belowgrade, the systems maximize property usage and meet your specific water quantity requirements. Corrugated Aluminum detention systems are available in all AASHTO M-196 and M-197 Types.

INFILTRATION

Corrugated Aluminum pipe and pipe-arch is available fully or partially perforated to meet your Low Impact Development (LID) requirements. Standard pipe-wall perforations (3/8” diameter holes meeting AASHTO M-36, Class 2) provide approximately 2.5% open area. Subsurface perforated Corrugated Aluminum infiltration systems store stormwater runoff in the pipe and surrounding stone during a storm until it can be slowly released into the surrounding native soil.

DURABILITY

  • THE UNIQUE COMBINATIONS of properties provided by aluminum and its alloys make aluminum one of the most versatile, economical, and attractive metallic materials for a broad range of uses.
  • Aluminum resists the kind of progressive oxidization that causes steel to rust away. The exposed surface of aluminum combines with oxygen to form an inert aluminum oxide film only a few ten-millionths of an inch thick, which blocks further oxidation. And, unlike iron rust, the aluminum oxide film does not flake off to expose a fresh surface to further oxidation. If the protective layer of aluminum is scratched, it will instantly reseal itself.
  • Appropriately alloyed and treated, aluminum can resist corrosion by water, salt, and other environmental factors, and by a wide range of other chemical and physical agents.

Pond Riser Structures

Economical. Environmentally friendly.

Vertical outlet pipes (also called "risers") are used to set the primary spillway water elevation in a pond or water impondment.

The trash guard shown on top of this riser forces the water in the pond to flow under the trash rack and at the same time blocks floating debris from discharging through the top of the riser. The inverted elbow shown on the riser is a mechanism by which the top portion of the impoundment can be discharged over a long period of time.

Permanent pond risers and their outlet discharge pipes are usually specified Aluminum which has excellent corrosion resistance.

Trash Racks

Rust-resistant. Low maintenance. Durable.

Our trash racks are manufactured using Aluminum Rebar or Corrugated Aluminum Pipe and can have a variety of shapes and/or sizes to fit on almost any structure.
